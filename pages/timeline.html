Timeline

The year 2019 as a whole was a huge year for Ethereum. Decentralized Finance (DeFi) really took off during this time. Medium offers a very detailed article about Ethereum in 2019 in regard to its exponential growth and why it still has a long way to go before peaking. PROGRESSION

The middle of 2020 marked a monumental event for crypto for the United States, and the world as an after-effect. In July, the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC) published a letter clarifying national banks' and federal savings associations' authority to provide cryptocurrency custody services for customers. The OCC granted banks the permission needed to provide custody services for crypto assets. This is a vital event toward mainstream adoption of crypto. Banks have long fought against crypto as the speed and security of crypto transactions makes the Banks' obsolete Swift system look like an aging dinosaur. As more banks finally embrace crypto, it will become more valuable. The third-quarter of 2020 was marked by turbulence in the DeFi arena. Decentralized Exchanges (DEX) that were trading in the single-digit millions at the start of the year surpassed $1 billion in a single day, exceeding that of centralized giants. Projects that were spun up in a matter of days were locking up nine-figures worth of value as great amounts of capital freely flowed to the next highest yielding farm. Too many ideas came into play, regardless of how bright they were, and DeFi tokens tumbled. Top DEX Uniswap suffered a setback. Messari provided a very detailed report on this situation. If you check it out and scroll to Page 12, you will see the high gas prices (transaction ) that occurred due to the boom in the DeFi sector.
